FFF845 League Constitution

  1. League Basics
  1. Money
  1. Entry fee is $150[a]
  2. Payout is 63.33% ($1140) to first place, 20% ($360) to second place, and 8.33% ($150) to third place, and 8.33% ($150) to the highest scoring regular season team.
  3. The entry fee can change upon a majority league vote.
  1. 12 Teams.
  1. We can add 2 teams if there is a 75% league vote.[b]
  2. Owners can be expelled from the league with a 100% (expelled owner excluded) vote, and only with a new owner already agreeing to join.
  1. Communication
  1. All league members must be included within the league’s group chat.
  1. The Game
  1. Scoring
  1. Passing
  1. 4 pts for TD
  2. .04 pt for every yard
  3. 2 pts for two point conversion
  4. -2 pts for interception
  1. Rushing
  1. 6 pts for TD
  2. .1 pt for every yard
  3. 2 pts for two point conversion
  1. Receiving
  1. 6 pts for TD
  2. .1 pt for every yard
  3. .5 pts per reception
  4. 2 pts for two point conversion
  1. Miscellaneous
  1. -2 pts for a fumble
  2. 6 pts for kickoff return TD
  3. 6 pts for punt return TD
  4. 6 pts for fumble recovered for TD
  1. Defense / Special Teams
  1. 1 pt for each sack
  2. 6 pts for fumble return TD
  3. 6 pts for interception return TD
  4. 6 pts for kickoff return TD
  5. 6 pts for punt return TD
  6. 6 pts for block punt / field goal for TD
  7. 2 pts for block punt, PAT, or FG
  8. 2 pts for each fumble recovered
  9. 2 pts for each safety
  10. 10 pts for 0 points allowed
  11. 7 pts for 1-6 points allowed
  12. 4 pts for 7-13 points allowed
  13. 1 pt for 14-17 points allowed
  14. 0 pts for 18-27 points allowed
  15. -3 pts for 28-34 points allowed
  16. -7 pts for 35-45 points allowed
  17. -10 pts for 46+ points allowed

  1. Roster
  1. 1 QB, 2 RB, 2 WR, 1 FLEX[c] (RB/WR/TE) 1 OP (QB/RB/WR/TE), 1 TE, 1 D/ST, 5 BE
  1. Schedule
  1. 13 regular season weeks
  1. No tie breakers in the event of a tie
  1. Each team plays every team at least once.
  2. 3 playoff weeks
  1. 1 week per round
  2. Playoff seeding tie breaker is Total Points For
  3. Playoff matchup tie breaker is Playing Seeding
  4. The winners of each division will receive play in the first week without any risk of elimination.
  5. The winner of the division champions week 1 playoff matchup will be able to select their week 2 playoff matchup amongst the remaining teams, aside from the other divisional winner just faced.[d]
  6. The winner of the division champions week 1 playoff matchup must make his selection via a video named “The Decision” by the following Wednesday at 10pm EST.
  1. Transactions and Keepers
  1. Free Agents
  1. Players are acquired using the FAAB system
  2. FAAB runs every day Wednesday through Monday at 11am
  3. NFL weeks begin Thursday and end Monday night, meaning all free agent players become locked from the moment their game kicks off until Tuesday.
  4. Each owner will start the year with $100 FAAB
  5. The tie breaker will be a waiver order which resets each week to the inverse order of the standings
  6. The minimum bid for a player is $0
  7. Upon the outset of the playoffs, all eliminated teams will be barred from making free agent transactions[e]
  1. Trading
  1. Owners may trade players and FAAB dollars
  2. All proposed commodities being traded and any other provisions to the trade must be traded / executed at the time of the trade being accepted (ex: promising to trade a player back later is ILLEGAL)
  3. All trades are subject to a 1 day review period by the commissioner
  4. Trades can be vetoed only by the Commish
  1. Trades should only be vetoed if there is suspected collusion
  1. Ex. Giving $10 USD for a player.
  1. Trades will not be vetoed due to perceived “unfairness”, as long as both owners made the trade to legitimately better their team, that trade is sanctioned.
  1. Ex. Shane trading Dez Bryant for Johnny Manziel, Any of Nick’s trades, etc.
  1. The trade deadline will be the Wednesday preceding Thanksgiving.
  1. Keepers
  1. Owners are allows one keeper from the previous year’s draft
  1. Owner must have personally drafted player
  2. Keeper can only be kept for one season
  3. The price of the keeper in the season they are kept will be 150% of their drafted value from the previous season, rounded up (ex. if you draft Sammy Watkins for $19, he is keepable the next season for $29)[f]
  1. The Draft
  1. The draft is an auction format
  2. Each team will be given a $200 budget to spend
  1. Offseason
  1. Transactions
  1. Offseason trading is not allowed
  1. Rules & Voting (2014 Rev)
  1. Every year a rules proposal Google Moderator will be created as a forum for people to propose & discuss new rules / ideas
  2. The most popular / compelling ideas will be placed on a ballot for voting using the following criteria
  1. More upvotes than downvotes
  2. 75% of league up/down voted / add to discussion
  1. New rules must receive 75% majority vote (9/12) to pass[g]
  2. Commish has final say on controversies.
  3. Loopholes in the constitution needing immedeate decisions can be voted on within the group chat, with the majority (excluding the affected party(s) need to approve. Commissioner or competition (if time permits) wins the vote.
  1. Draft Date
  1. The draft date (and location?) will be decided every year by the final week of July
  2. Drafts should begin no later than 8:30PM if online (Auctions take a while)
  1. Payments
  1. Teams must pay and commit to returning to the league by August 1st
  2. All controversy regarding payment will be decided upon by the treasurer first, and the commissioner if the treasurer doesn’t exist or abstains.
  1.  The Keller-Cogan Bowl Rules
  1. The game will take place the Saturday or Sunday morning after Thanksgiving.
  2. The two divisions, decided upon before the start of the season, face off for points in their fantasy football matchup that week.
  1. If a player is absent, both teams in the matchup are ineligible to receive points in their matchup.
  1. The division who has the most accumulative points starts with the ball.
  2. The game is to be played at Clarkstown High School North, on the turf football field
  3. The game is to be played sideline to sideline, using the sidelines as the start of the goal-line, and the yellow line behind it as the end of the goal-line.
  1. The field is to be 30 yards wide.
  1. The game is to be played to 15 touchdowns, win by 2. The only first down marker is to be placed at midfield.
  2. There is one blitz allowed per set of downs, conversely, there is one QB rush allowed per set of downs.
  3. This is a tackle game, yes, even Shane. You have the option to opt out and be wrap-up, but don’t be soft.
  4. When facing a 4th down, the offense has the option to “punt”, which sets the defense up with the ball at their own numbers.
  5. In the case of a game-ending injury, either a player from the healthy team takes the injured player to medical assistance, or the game goes on with the healthy team using a sub.
